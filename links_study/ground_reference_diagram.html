2. Why DC− (Not AC−) Must Be the Control Board Reference

⚠ CRITICAL: AC− is NOT suitable as a ground reference for a non-isolated PFC. Here's why:

3. Detailed Explanation

3.1 The Problem with AC−

In a non-isolated boost PFC, the AC input is rectified (either by a diode bridge or totem-pole sync rectifiers). The AC− node after the EMI filter is not a stable DC potential. During the positive half-cycle of mains, AC− is near earth potential; during the negative half-cycle, it swings to approximately −325 Vpk relative to earth.

If you reference your control board to AC−:

  • The DSP's AGND would swing ±325 V at 50 Hz relative to earth ground
  • All signal cables (JTAG, UART, USB) connecting to the DSP would see this enormous CM voltage
  • The Y-capacitors in the EMI filter would pass significant 50 Hz leakage current through the control ground path
  • Any oscilloscope probe connected to a DSP pin would create a direct short through the scope's earth-referenced ground lead

3.2 Why DC− is the Correct Choice

DC− is the output return of the PFC stage. It is the node that all output-side measurements (voltage, current) reference to. By connecting the control board AGND to DC− at a single star point:

  • The control board ground is at the same DC potential as all sensed signals
  • Isolated gate drivers handle the PWM signals crossing to the high-side SiC MOSFET (whose source is at the switching node, not at DC−)
  • Isolated amplifiers (AMC1311, AMC1302) have their HV-side ground (GND1) tied to DC−, and their LV-side ground (GND2) tied to the control board AGND
  • The isolation barrier absorbs the CM voltage between the switching node and DC−

3.3 Star-Point Connection Details

The star point between DC− and control board AGND should be implemented as:

  • For prototyping: A 0 Ω jumper resistor or a single short trace connecting the two ground planes at exactly one point
  • For production: A dedicated copper tie between the HV power ground plane and the LV control ground plane, placed near the auxiliary power supply return
  • Location: Place the star point near where the auxiliary isolated DC-DC converter's secondary-side ground connects — this is typically close to the isolated amplifiers and DSP
📌 Note: The control board should have separate AGND (analog ground) and DGND (digital ground) regions that are joined at a single point underneath the DSP. The DC− star point connects to this combined ground at the DSP location.

6. Key Design Rules

  1. Never reference control ground to AC− — it floats at line frequency and creates lethal CM voltages
  2. DC− is the only valid power ground reference for a non-isolated PFC output stage
  3. All isolation barriers must be respected — no copper traces, no thermal pads, no component bodies crossing the barrier except designated isolators
  4. Single star point between DC− and control AGND — no multiple connections that create ground loops
  5. AGND and DGND join under the DSP — analog and digital grounds meet at the converter's ground pin
  6. PE (protective earth) connects to chassis/enclosure only — never to the control board ground
  7. Creepage/clearance: Maintain ≥8 mm creepage for 400 V DC bus (per IEC 60950-1 / IEC 62368-1)
