400 V DC Bus Voltage Sensing Circuit

Sensing the 400 V DC bus using a resistor divider feeding an AMC1311 high-impedance isolated amplifier, producing a 0–2 V output compatible with the TMS320F280039C ADC input range (0–3.3 V).

1. Design Requirements

ParameterValue
Maximum DC bus voltage to sense400 V DC (nominal), up to 450 V DC (max transient)
AMC1311 input range±2 V (differential), high-impedance input (Rin = 31.2 kΩ typ)

2. Calculation Steps

Step 1: Determine Divider Ratio

Vin(AMC1311) = Vbus × Rin / (Rdiv + Rin)

Where Rin = 31.2 kΩ (AMC1311 internal input resistance, differential)
Rdiv = total series divider resistance = 634 kΩ

At Vbus = 400 V:
Vin = 400 × 31.2k / (634k + 31.2k) = 400 × 31.2 / 665.2 = 18.82 mV

AMC1311 output: Vout = 2 × Vin = 2 × 18.82 mV = 37.64 mV
Wait — this is far too small! The TIDA-010054 uses 634 kΩ for a different voltage range. We need to recalculate for our 400 V application to maximize the ADC resolution.

Step 2: Recalculate for Optimal ADC Utilization

We want the AMC1311 output to reach ≈2 V at maximum bus voltage (450 V) to utilize the full ADC range. Working backwards:

Target: Vout(AMC1311) = 2.0 V at Vbus(max) = 450 V
Vin(AMC1311) = Vout / 2 = 2.0 / 2 = 1.0 V

But AMC1311 maximum differential input is ±2 V, and the common-mode input range is limited.
For safety and margin, target Vin = 1.0 V at Vbus = 450 V (50% of AMC1311 max).

Divider ratio needed: Rin / (Rdiv + Rin) = 1.0 / 450
31.2k / (Rdiv + 31.2k) = 1/450
Rdiv + 31.2k = 31.2k × 450 = 14,040 kΩ
Rdiv = 14,040k − 31.2k = 14,008.8 kΩ ≈ 14 MΩ
That's a very large divider resistance, which means very low current and good power efficiency. However, the TIDA-010054 uses 634 kΩ, so let's design for that constraint and accept a smaller output signal, then add a buffer/offset stage.

Step 3: Design with 634 kΩ Divider Chain (TIDA-010054 Approach)

The TIDA-010054 uses 634 kΩ total for sensing up to 1500 V DC. For our 400 V application:

Rdiv = 634 kΩ (split as 2 × 317 kΩ for voltage rating)
Rin = 31.2 kΩ (AMC1311 internal)

Divider ratio = 31.2k / (634k + 31.2k) = 31.2 / 665.2 = 0.04691

At Vbus = 400 V: Vin = 400 × 0.04691 = 18.76 mV
AMC1311 Vout = 2 × 18.76 mV = 37.53 mV

At Vbus = 450 V (max): Vin = 450 × 0.04691 = 21.11 mV
AMC1311 Vout = 2 × 21.11 mV = 42.22 mV
This output is very small (37–42 mV). The AMC1311 is designed for ±2 V input. With only 18–21 mV at the input, we're using only ~1% of its range. This is the TIDA-010054 approach for high-voltage (1500 V) sensing. For 400 V, we should use a lower divider resistance to get more signal.

Step 4: Optimized Design for 400 V Bus

Let's design a divider that gives Vin = 1.0 V at Vbus = 400 V (50% of AMC1311 full scale):

Vin(target) = 1.0 V at Vbus = 400 V
Idivider = Vin / Rin = 1.0 / 31.2k = 32.05 µA
Rtotal = Vbus / Idivider = 400 / 32.05µ = 12.48 MΩ
Rdiv = Rtotal − Rin = 12.48M − 31.2k ≈ 12.45 MΩ

Split into 2 equal resistors: R1 = R2 = 12.45M / 2 = 6.225 MΩ each
Use standard E96 values: R1 = R2 = 6.20 MΩ (0805 or 1206, 0.25 W each)

Verification with 6.20 MΩ + 6.20 MΩ:

Rdiv = 12.40 MΩ
Vin at 400 V = 400 × 31.2k / (12,400k + 31.2k) = 400 × 31.2 / 12,431.2 = 1.004 V ✓
Vin at 450 V = 450 × 31.2 / 12,431.2 = 1.130 V (within ±2 V limit) ✓
AMC1311 Vout at 400 V = 2 × 1.004 = 2.008 V ✓
AMC1311 Vout at 450 V = 2 × 1.130 = 2.260 V ✓
ADC reading at 400 V = (2.008 / 3.3) × 4095 = 2,489 counts (60.7% of full scale) ✓
✅ Final Design: R1 = R2 = 6.20 MΩ (1206, 0.25 W). AMC1311 output = 2.008 V at 400 V bus. DSP ADC reads ≈2,489 of 4,095 counts.

3. Power Dissipation & Voltage Rating Calculations

Power Dissipation per Resistor

Idivider = 400 V / 12,431.2 kΩ = 32.18 µA

PR1 = I² × R1 = (32.18µ)² × 6.20M = 6.43 mW
PR2 = I² × R2 = 6.43 mW
PRin(AMC1311) = I² × 31.2k = 0.033 mW

Total divider power = 6.43 + 6.43 = 12.86 mW (negligible)

Voltage Rating per Resistor

VR1 = I × R1 = 32.18µ × 6.20M = 199.5 V
VR2 = I × R2 = 199.5 V

At 450 V max transient: VR1 = VR2 = 450 × 6.20M / 12,431.2k = 224.4 V

Required voltage rating per resistor: ≥ 250 V (with margin)
Recommended: 1206 package, 200 V working voltage minimum
For safety: use resistors rated for ≥ 500 V pulse (e.g., Vishay MCT 0603 or higher)

4. Anti-Aliasing Filter

The AMC1311 output needs a low-pass anti-aliasing filter before the DSP ADC.
The DSP ADC sampling rate: typically 100 kSPS to 1 MSPS for PFC control.

For a 20 kHz PFC switching frequency, target corner frequency fc = 50 kHz:

RC low-pass: Raa = 100 Ω, Caa = ?
fc = 1 / (2π × Raa × Caa)
Caa = 1 / (2π × 100 × 50k) = 31.8 nF
Use standard value: Caa = 33 nF (X7R, 0603)

Actual fc = 1 / (2π × 100 × 33n) = 48.2 kHz ✓
