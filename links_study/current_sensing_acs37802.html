DC Output Current Sensing — ACS37802 Hall-Effect Sensor

Sensing the DC output current (up to 20 A) of the 400 V PFC using an Allegro ACS37802 (or ACS37002) Hall-effect current sensor IC with integrated conductor, providing galvanic isolation and a ratiometric analog output for the DSP ADC.

Important: The ACS37802 output at +30 A would be 3.45 V, which exceeds the 3.3 V supply rail. The output will clip at approximately VCC − 0.1 V = 3.2 V. For 20 A nominal operation, the output is 2.85 V — well within range. If bidirectional current measurement is needed (e.g., for regenerative modes), the ±30 A range is appropriate. For unidirectional-only sensing, the ACS37802-030B5 (unidirectional, 0–30 A) would be better.

3. Calculation Steps

Step 1: Verify Output Voltage Range

Vout = VCC/2 + Sensitivity × Isense

At I = 0 A: Vout = 3.3/2 + 60m × 0 = 1.650 V
At I = +10 A: Vout = 1.650 + 0.600 = 2.250 V
At I = +20 A: Vout = 1.650 + 1.200 = 2.850 V
At I = +25 A: Vout = 1.650 + 1.500 = 3.150 V
At I = +30 A: Vout = 1.650 + 1.800 = 3.450 V → clips at ~3.2 V
At I = −10 A: Vout = 1.650 − 0.600 = 1.050 V
At I = −20 A: Vout = 1.650 − 1.200 = 0.450 V

Step 2: ADC Resolution

ADC range used for 0 to +20 A: 1.650 V to 2.850 V = 1.200 V swing
ADC codes for 20 A range: (1.200 / 3.3) × 4095 = 1,489 counts
Resolution: 20 A / 1489 = 13.4 mA/count
Effective resolution: log₂(1489) = 10.5 bits

For full bidirectional range (−20 A to +20 A):
Total swing: 2.850 − 0.450 = 2.400 V
ADC codes: (2.400 / 3.3) × 4095 = 2,978 counts
Resolution: 40 A / 2978 = 13.4 mA/count
Effective resolution: 11.5 bits

Step 3: Power Dissipation in Sensor Conductor

Rcond = 1.2 mΩ (ACS37802 internal conductor)
Pcond = I² × Rcond = 20² × 0.0012 = 0.48 W
Pcond at 25 A peak: 25² × 0.0012 = 0.75 W

Thermal rise: ACS37802 package thermal resistance θJA ≈ 30°C/W
ΔT at 20 A: 0.48 × 30 = 14.4°C above ambient ✓
ΔT at 25 A: 0.75 × 30 = 22.5°C above ambient ✓

Step 4: Anti-Aliasing Filter

The ACS37802 output has inherent bandwidth of 240 kHz.
For PFC current control at 20 kHz switching, target fc = 50 kHz.

RC low-pass: Raa = 100 Ω, Caa = ?
fc = 1/(2π × Raa × Caa) = 50 kHz
Caa = 1/(2π × 100 × 50k) = 31.8 nF → use 33 nF
fc(actual) = 1/(2π × 100 × 33n) = 48.2 kHz ✓

7. Connection to DSP SD Input Port

The ACS37802 produces an analog voltage output, not a bitstream. Therefore it connects to the DSP's regular ADC input (ADCINA1), not the SD filter module. The SD filter module is only used with the AMC1302 (delta-sigma modulator).

Key distinction: The SD (Sigma-Delta) filter port on the C2000 DSP is specifically designed for the AMC1302's 1-bit bitstream output. The ACS37802 and LEM sensors produce analog voltages that go to the regular SAR ADC channels. Both are available on the F280039C simultaneously.
